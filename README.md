# Timac Fuel Station Sales & Performance Analysis
###### Project Overview

This project analyzes sales and operational performance data from Timac Fuel Stations with the goal of identifying revenue drivers, demand patterns, and operational efficiencies. Using Excel for data preparation and Power BI for visualization, the analysis provides actionable insights to support revenue optimization and decision-making.

###### Objectives

The analysis seeks to:

Evaluate overall revenue performance across time, stations, and products

Identify top-performing products and stations

Analyze shift and supervisor performance

Examine price, demand, and seasonal trends

Provide data-driven insights through interactive dashboards

###### Key Insights

Petrol is the dominant revenue contributor across all stations.

Morning shifts consistently generate higher revenue and transaction counts.

Certain stations and supervisors outperform others, indicating best-practice opportunities.

January records peak fuel demand, suggesting strong seasonal effects.

Weekday sales exceed weekend sales due to business and transportation activities.

###### Key Assumptions & Limitations

Revenue was used as a proxy for fuel volume due to lack of litre data.

Analysis assumes stable pricing within short periods.

Insights are based solely on the available dataset and timeframe.



###### Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates how transactional sales data can be transformed into actionable business intelligence using Excel and Power BI. The insights generated can support pricing strategies, operational planning, and performance management for fuel retail operations.
